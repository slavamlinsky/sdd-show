## A working definition

**Spec driven development** means you treat **clear specifications** as the primary input to building software: you write down intent, behavior, and acceptance **before** (or tightly alongside) the code that implements them.

That is different from “we have Jira tickets, so we have specs.” Ticket titles are not specs.

## What a good spec anchors

- **Scope** — what is in and out for this slice of work.
- **Behavior** — what happens for normal paths and important edge cases.
- **Acceptance** — how you know you are done, in testable language.

## Why bother

SDD reduces expensive rework: fewer “I thought you meant…” moments, fewer surprise integrations, and a shared story your future self can read.

## What SDD is not

It is not heavyweight documentation for its own sake. The goal is **clarity and alignment**, not page count. Start small: one page that everyone agrees is true beats five scattered chat threads.
