## 1. Frame the problem

Write **one short problem statement**: who is blocked, what outcome you want, and what is explicitly out of scope for now.

## 2. Draft acceptance

Turn the outcome into **3–7 acceptance criteria** you could verify in a demo. If you cannot phrase them clearly, you are not ready to estimate or build.

## 3. Sketch the design at the right depth

For UI flows: wire or bullet steps. For APIs: request/response shapes and error cases. Match depth to risk — deeper where bugs are expensive.

## 4. Implement against the spec

Keep the spec nearby. When reality teaches you something new, **update the spec** — that is how the document stays trustworthy.

## 5. Demo and prune

Show the criteria being met. Remove or archive details that nobody needed. **Living** means useful, not large.
