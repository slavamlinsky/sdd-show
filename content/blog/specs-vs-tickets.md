## Tickets are for flow

**Tickets** (cards, issues) are great for **assignment, status, and sequencing**. They answer: who is doing what, and what stage is it in?

## Specs are for meaning

**Specs** explain **what “done” means** in enough detail that different people would build roughly the same thing. They answer: what should the system do, and how do we agree it works?

## The failure mode

When tickets replace specs, you get fast movement with unclear targets. Everyone is “busy,” but rework piles up because the shared picture lived in chat.

## The fix

Pair every meaningful ticket with **linked acceptance** — even a few lines in the issue body, or a link to a short doc. The spec can be tiny; it cannot be implicit.

## Summary

Use **tickets to run the board** and **specs to run the product**. The best teams connect both so traceability is obvious with minimal ceremony.
